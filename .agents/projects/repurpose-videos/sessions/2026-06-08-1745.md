# Session Log: 2026-06-08 17:45

## Context
Further optimization of the video repurposing pipeline to resolve two user feedback issues:
1. Subtitle removal masking was blocky/rectangular ("not good as before") and did not target the characters cleanly.
2. The original talker's voice was still present, and they wanted a karaoke-style background track without vocals.
3. Pre-cached the EasyOCR models in Docker to prevent deployment startup latency.

## Changes Made

1. **Stroke-Level Subtitle Masking (`repurpose.py`)**:
   - Replaced the full bounding box box/blur rectangle masking with a precise stroke-level mask.
   - Used a `13x13` ellipse kernel to dilate character strokes, ensuring they completely cover the character boundaries and their black outlines/glow (which previously caused black smudge/ghosting artifacts).
   - Restricted the stroke-mask copying to the detected text contours' bounding boxes (with a small 8px padding) to protect other non-text white graphic elements on the screen.
   - For `inpaint` mode, applied `cv2.inpaint` using Navier-Stokes (`INPAINT_NS`) or Telea with the precise stroke mask, yielding beautiful, near-invisible character erasures.
   - For `contour` mode, blended the blurred segment and original frame using the precise stroke mask to only blur the characters instead of drawing a blocky blurred rectangular band.

2. **Mandatory Karaoke-style Vocal Removal (`repurpose.py`)**:
   - Moved the Demucs vocal separation step outside the optional Siren TTS voiceover block.
   - The pipeline now always separates the vocals from the background music/SFX, mapping only the isolated BGM track (`no_vocals.wav`) to the final video output (re-encoded to AAC). This guarantees that the original talker's voice is completely silenced, leaving a clean karaoke-style track.
   - Kept the original audio fallback only if vocal separation fails or is skipped.

3. **Docker Cache Warm Up (`Dockerfile`)**:
   - Added `'vi'` to the EasyOCR cache warm up step to ensure all Vietnamese OCR models are pre-cached inside the Docker image during building.
